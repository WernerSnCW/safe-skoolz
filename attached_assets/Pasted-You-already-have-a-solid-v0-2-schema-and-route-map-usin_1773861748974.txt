You already have a solid v0.2 schema and route map; using it as-is you’re in a good place to build a minimal but LOPIVI‑aligned ECMS that can also output Convivèxit and Machista case bundles.

Where this design is strong
Clear separation of incidents vs protocols, which matches how LOPIVI and Balearic guidance distinguish “concern / possible bullying” from a formally opened protocol.

Pattern alerts and overdue checks map neatly to the “early detection” and “rapid, coordinated response” language in Convivèxit and LOPIVI (risk mapping, early indicators, time‑bound actions).

Audit log, SAR export, retention rules and encryption at rest/in transit line up with GDPR and LOPIVI’s emphasis on traceability and child‑centred case management.

Gaps / refinements to consider
LOPIVI and Balearic protocols require a designated delegado/coordinator role and clear governance; you may want a designations or roles_per_school table to record appointments, training dates, and mandate scope rather than relying only on users.role.

Convivèxit annexes are explicit; you’ve started this with annex_reference and export bundles, but you might add a small lookup table annex_templates (id, framework, annex_code, title, description) so exports can stay in sync when the CAIB updates forms.

For Machista Violence, protocols usually track contact with external specialist services (IB Dona, municipal services, etc.); consider normalising external_referral_body into a referral_bodies table so schools can configure local contacts per island/municipality.

Possible small schema additions

delegated_roles table: {id, school_id, user_id, role_type ('lopivi_delegate','convivexit_coordinator','machista_protocol_lead'), appointed_at, expires_at} to prove compliance during inspections.

case_tasks table (optional but powerful): {id, school_id, protocol_id, task_type, due_at, completed_at, assignee_id}, fed by your overdue_checks rules instead of encoding everything only in jobs; this gives human‑readable “to do” lists.
