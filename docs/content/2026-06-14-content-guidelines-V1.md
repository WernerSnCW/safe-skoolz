- **Spelling is `vibez` with a Z** — NOT "Vibes". Lowercase wordmark (Kenyan Coffee). Per-tenant = **"{School} vibez"** (e.g. "Morna vibez").
- **Domain = `schoolvibez.com`** — SchoolVBE retires as a spoken brand and schoolvbe.com is superseded.
- **VBE** = the cause/framework (free, for everyone — Values-Based Education).
- **vibez** = the software platform + the parent community within it. The Accord brief's "VIBE" = vibez (same thing, capitalised there). Internally the code keeps `voice_*` for the coalition; users see "{School} vibez" / "your voice".
- **"VAD" (Values-Aligned Dialogue)** — recommended KILLED as parent-facing jargon. The PTA framework is publicly "an open, fair way of running the PTA"; so **G2** reads "the PTA adopts an open operating structure," not "adopts VAD." (Tom to confirm whether VAD survives as an internal term.)

## GL2 — Scope: broad cause, two-goal ask
- The **proposition is broad** (values-based education for your child's school — what parents emotionally join).
- The **mandate/ask is exactly two goals (G1 + G2)**, stated plainly at the join/mandate moment: *"vibez will speak to your school about two things — that it adopts VBE, and that the PTA runs openly — nothing else."*
- **G1** = school adopts VBE. **G2** = PTA adopts an open operating structure.
- vibez is **NOT a general complaints channel.** Broader topics only re-enter *after recognition*, via the §8 engagement layer (Questions gated to legal obligations, Ideas to a community vote) — never open-ended parent grievance.
- **Verifiable criterion:** every parent-facing page classifies cleanly as "the cause" (broad VBE) or "the ask" (G1/G2 mandate); zero pages promise open-ended advocacy or a complaints channel.

## GL3 — Never adversarial — to the school OR the PTA
- The leverage exists *operationally* but is **never framed as beating anyone.** Every frame is "make it easy for the school / PTA to say yes."
- The **PTA is a partner to be helped** (G2 = help it become a genuinely representative body), never an incumbent to outnumber, replace, or defeat.
- The legitimacy number is **"N families now share this hope,"** never "we outnumber the PTA."
- **No-confidence** stays FAQ-only, neutral governance fact, never a CTA.
- The only permitted leverage statement is the **administrative-burden** one ("if the school doesn't engage collectively, each parent communicates individually — more work for everyone").
- **Verifiable criterion:** a tone/banned-language pass — no parent-facing string uses *outnumber / beat / takeover / force / demand / pressure / no-confidence-as-action*, toward either the school or the PTA. (Reinforces the existing "support the school, the PTA, and each other; criticise none" principle.)

## GL4 — Law-citing / behaviour-pattern content: ordinary care, no special gate
- **There is NO live Morna litigation** (a prior memory premise was wrong — corrected 2026-06-14). No heavyweight legal-review gate is needed.
- Covered by GL2 (§10 legal-obligation questions are deferred to Chapter 4 + stay generic/framework-level, never an incident or individual) + GL3 (non-adversarial) + two ordinary disciplines: **get legal citations factually correct** (they're claims about law) and **name patterns, not people** (defamation hygiene on Track-C behaviour-pattern content).

## GL5 — Private-first remediation (the compliance concierge)
- Any gap VIBE spots between a school's documents/policies and its legal obligations is surfaced **privately to the school first, constructively, with time to respond** — the community is never used to blindside or expose the school.
- Public transparency reports only the **positive end-state** ("the school has published X"), never "the school was non-compliant."
- **Mechanism (future feature — a school-onboarding / compliance phase, NOT Chapter 2; captured in the Chapter-4 engagement IP):** school shares its governing bodies + regulations + current policy docs → AI-assisted review (skills/agents) against the legal-obligation map produces private feedback/suggestions → school gets a response window → only then does anything surface to the community, framed positively. Makes VIBE a partner that helps the school look good, not a watchdog.

## GL6 — Three-depth (L1/L2/L3) applied selectively, at the decisions that matter
- The L1/L2/L3 model (headline / exec summary / full detail) is a sound principle but is **NOT authored for every piece** (that's 3× content cost; the brief itself says most parents stop at L1).
- **L1 (headline)** — always (nearly free). **L2 + L3** — only where the stakes are real:
  - **(a) the big decision points** a parent is asked to make — supporting **VBE (G1)**, backing **PTA reform (G2)**, and the **join / mandate authorisation** (so the decision is genuinely informed — ties to GL5).
  - **(b) the rights / compliance cluster** — parent rights, the school's published commitments, the legal-obligation answers.
- Routine / marketing / community-update content stays L1-ish (L2 only where it clearly helps); no L3.
- **Verifiable criterion:** the content inventory tags each surface with the levels it actually needs; L2/L3 exist only on the decision points + rights/compliance cluster.
