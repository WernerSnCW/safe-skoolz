## 2. The VBE Parent Voice Framework

VIBES has developed a **parent voice framework** — a simple commitment that **every term** the PTA will:

1. **Gather feedback** from all year groups.
2. **Meet** with school leadership.
3. **Publish a summary** of themes raised.
4. **Publish actions** agreed.
5. **Report back** on progress.

> That simple cycle can transform a PTA from being seen as a fundraising committee into being
> seen as a genuine representative body for the whole school community.

## 3. The Role of Effective Communication Between a PTA and the School Community (essay)

### Introduction
A Parent-Teacher Association (PTA) exists to strengthen the relationship between families, students, staff and school leadership. While fundraising often receives the most attention, one of the most valuable functions of a PTA is acting as a communication bridge between the school and the parent community.

When communication is effective, parents feel informed, heard and involved. Schools gain valuable insight into the needs and perspectives of families. Students ultimately benefit from a stronger, more collaborative school community.

### The PTA as a Communication Bridge
The PTA occupies a unique position within the school community. It is neither part of the school's management structure nor simply a parent social group. Instead, it can serve as a trusted intermediary that facilitates communication and understanding.

An effective PTA helps:
- Communicate school initiatives to parents.
- Gather feedback from parents.
- Identify common themes and concerns.
- Share constructive suggestions with school leadership.
- Report outcomes and actions back to families.
- Build trust and transparency across the community.

The PTA should not act as a complaints department or an opposition group. Its role is to facilitate understanding, collaboration and positive engagement.

### The Communication Cycle
The most successful PTAs operate a continuous communication cycle.

**Step 1: Inform** — The school shares information about: school priorities; upcoming events; new initiatives; changes to policies or procedures; opportunities for parent involvement. The PTA helps communicate these messages in a clear, accessible and parent-friendly manner.

**Step 2: Listen** — The PTA actively gathers feedback from parents. This may include: surveys; parent forums; coffee mornings; informal conversations; year-group representatives; digital suggestion forms. The aim is to identify trends and common themes rather than focusing on isolated individual concerns.

**Step 3: Represent** — The PTA collates feedback and presents it to school leadership in a constructive and balanced way. This approach focuses on solutions rather than complaints.

**Step 4: Feedback** — After discussions with the school, the PTA communicates outcomes back to parents. For example: what issues were raised; what actions have been agreed; what changes may be considered; why certain decisions have been made. Closing the loop is essential. Parents are more likely to engage when they know their views have been heard and considered.

### Representing the Whole School Community
One of the greatest challenges for any PTA is ensuring that it represents all families rather than only the most vocal or active members. A PTA should strive to hear from: parents of different year groups; long-term and new families; working and non-working parents; families from different cultural backgrounds; students with additional learning needs; parents who rarely attend school events. A PTA should actively seek out quieter voices, not simply rely on those who regularly participate.

### Practical Methods for Gathering Feedback
- **Parent Surveys** — short surveys (communication effectiveness; event preferences; wellbeing concerns; engagement opportunities). Concise and easy to complete.
- **Year Group Representatives** — share information, gather feedback, identify common concerns, encourage participation; ensures all parts of the school are represented.
- **Open Forums** — occasional open meetings; emphasis on dialogue rather than debate.
- **Digital Suggestion Channels** — online forms; anonymous submissions can increase participation.

### Should a PTA Publish a Newsletter?
In most schools, yes. A regular PTA newsletter can become one of the most effective communication tools available. The purpose is not simply to announce fundraising but to strengthen community engagement and transparency. It might include: **Updates** (recent activities, meeting summaries, project progress); **Impact Reporting** (equipment purchased, activities funded, improvements completed); **Community Information** (upcoming events, volunteer opportunities, school initiatives, wellbeing resources); **Parent Voice** (themes emerging from feedback and how they're being addressed).

### Recommended Communication Channels
Email newsletter (formal updates); school app (reminders/events); WhatsApp groups (year-group, carefully moderated); social media (celebrating successes, events); PTA website or page (permanent home for info, documents, meeting summaries); in-person events (relationship-building, feedback). No single channel reaches everyone — a combination is required.

### Transparency and Trust
Trust grows when communication is honest, consistent, timely, respectful and transparent. Parents should understand: what the PTA is working on; what feedback has been received; what actions are being taken; how decisions are made.

### Measuring Success
Evaluate the communication strategy by asking: Do parents feel informed? Do parents feel heard? Are different year groups represented? Is participation increasing? Is communication two-way rather than one-way? Do parents understand the impact of PTA activities?

### Conclusion
The most effective PTAs do far more than raise money. They help create a connected, engaged and collaborative school community. By acting as a communication bridge between parents and the school, a PTA can strengthen trust, improve understanding, increase participation and ensure that families have a meaningful voice in school life. The ultimate beneficiaries are the students, who thrive when the adults around them work together in a spirit of openness, partnership and shared purpose.
